The difference in compression performance shown in \autoref{tab:performance-results-hlm-dw}, especially that of Landsat, are likely due to the characteristics of the specific images used. For example, the Landsat image only has six spectral bands, as opposed to 120 and 200 (?) for Hypso and AVIRIS.

\begin{table}[ht]
	\caption{PSNR of decompressed images averaged over ten coastline images from HYPSO-2 for a set of PAEs.}
	\label{tab:psnr-compared}
	\begin{center}
		\begin{tabular}{lrr}
			\hline
			\textbf{PAE} & \textbf{CR} & \textbf{PSNR (dB)} \\
			\hline
			0            & 1.995       & N/A                \\ % 2.03            -> 2.02
			1            & 2.488       & 225.86             \\ % 2.55 (225.86dB) -> 2.53 (225.86dB)
			3            & 3.077       & 207.95             \\ % 3.17 (207.95dB) -> 3.15 (207.94dB)
			7            & 3.912       & 192.55             \\ % 4.07 (192.54dB) -> 4.03 (192.54dB)
			15           & 5.219       & 178.08             \\ % 5.45 (178.10dB) -> 5.39 (178.10dB)
			\hline
		\end{tabular}
	\end{center}
\end{table}

Note that the delayed weight update scheme does not affect the PSNR of the reconstructed images. This is due to the maximum error feature of the algorithm. Only the compression performance is affected. Also, PSNR for the ten different images vary only slightly, $\pm0.01dB$. This is also due to max error feature.
